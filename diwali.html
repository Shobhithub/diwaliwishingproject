✨ About Festival of Lights ✨

1. Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most celebrated festivals in India. It marks the victory of good over evil and light over darkness.
2. The festival commemorates Lord Rama’s return to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana, symbolizing the triumph of righteousness. People celebrate Diwali by lighting diyas, decorating homes, bursting fireworks, and sharing sweets.
3. It also honors Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity, with prayers and offerings.
4. Diwali signifies new beginnings, happiness, and the importance of unity and positivity in life, spreading joy, love, and light among families and communities.
